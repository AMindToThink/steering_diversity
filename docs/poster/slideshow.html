Why Does This Matter?

Activation steering is used in AI safety to elicit targeted behaviors — e.g., making a model "think it's deployed" during evaluation.

But if steering reduces output diversity, steered evaluations may miss important behaviors the model would otherwise exhibit.

2 / 7
Main Result: Steering Reduces Coverage Gain (Pass@k)

Pass@k = probability at least 1 of k samples solves a problem. Requires outputs to be both diverse and correct.

Coverage gain = pass@k − pass@1, isolating the diversity benefit of multiple attempts.

On HumanEval+ (164 problems, n=100, Qwen2.5-1.5B, α=2), steering significantly reduces coverage gain at k=2–25 (omnibus p = 0.025).

3 / 7
Why Not Standard Metrics?

Standard metrics such as mean pairwise cosine distance and n-gram diversity score incoherent noise as equally diverse as meaningful text.

Within-prompt diversity stays flat even as outputs degenerate into gibberish at high steering scales.

This motivated pass@k: outputs must be both diverse and correct.

4 / 7
Novel Metric: In-Context Learning Diversity

Key idea: if responses are similar, a base model should be able to predict one given the others.

We measure how much each new response's surprise drops given previous responses. The total drop = excess entropy E (learnable structure).

A coherence term C ensures noise isn't rewarded. D = C × E gives a single diversity score.

5 / 7
ICL Metric: Mode Count Validation

E and D increase monotonically with mode count (recipe, poem, code, etc.).

ak curves take longer to reach their floor with more modes — confirming the metric distinguishes diversity levels.
