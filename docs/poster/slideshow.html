Why Does This Matter?
  • AI evaluators want to study model behaviors that they cannot properly elicit with prompting.
  • Activation steering modifies model internals to elicit targeted behaviors
  • Key safety use case: make models "think they're deployed" during evaluation so they act faithfully
  • If steering reduces output diversity, evaluations may miss dangerous behaviors
2 / 5
How We Measure Diversity
  • A key problem is how to measure diversity.
  • Pass@k measures the probability at least 1 of k samples solves a problem; requires outputs to be diverse and correct
  • Novel ICL metric captures that if responses are similar, a base model can predict one given the others; measures learnable structure between responses. Unlike with pass@k, model responses can be diverse without accomplishing some goal.
  • Both avoid rating incoherent noise as "high-diversity", unlike standard metrics (cosine distance, n-grams)

Results
Steering significantly reduces coverage gain (p = 0.025)
ICL metric correctly distinguishes diversity levels
4 / 5
Takeaways & Next Steps
  • Steered models are less diverse, reducing the chance that multiple samples cover different solutions
  • New ICL metric identifies semantic similarity between responses using in-context learning
  • Next: scale to more steering methods, steering behaviors, and diversity measures. Validate ICL metric on benchmarks and apply it to steered models
