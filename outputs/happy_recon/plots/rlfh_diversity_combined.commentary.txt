With all three metrics overlaid, the picture is clear:

1. N-gram cosine diversity is at ceiling (~0.997-1.0) for all scales. It cannot
   discriminate between steering levels for this data. Likely because these are
   long-form creative writing responses with inherently diverse vocabulary.

2. Distinct n-grams and expectation-adjusted distinct n-grams track very closely
   (within ~1% of each other), both showing the same flat-then-jump pattern.
   The expectation adjustment doesn't change the story here.

3. All three metrics are purely lexical/surface-level. They cannot detect whether
   steering is collapsing the semantic diversity of responses (e.g., all responses
   saying the same thing in different words). The model-based metrics (SentBERT,
   NLI) from rlfh-gen-div would address this but require GPU to run in reasonable
   time.
