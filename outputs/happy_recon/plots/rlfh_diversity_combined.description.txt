Per-Input Diversity Metrics vs Steering Scale (all metrics overlaid)

All three per-input metrics on the same axes for comparison. Shows that:
  - N-gram cosine diversity sits near 1.0 for all scales -- essentially all
    responses look lexically distinct at the trigram level, making this metric
    uninformative for this dataset.
  - Distinct n-grams and expectation-adjusted distinct n-grams track closely,
    both showing a flat trend from scale 0-4 and a sharp jump at scale 8.
  - The scale-8 increase likely reflects the steered model producing more
    varied/incoherent text rather than genuinely more diverse content. Semantic
    metrics (SentBERT, NLI) would be needed to distinguish these cases.
